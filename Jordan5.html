Air Jordan 5

Release Date:1990

Price: $125.00 original retail price

The designer for this shoe is Tinker Hatfield

Pros

The Air Jordan 5 is known for its distinctive "shark tooth" midsole, clear plastic side panels, and high-quality leather that offers durability and comfort. While it is versatile and holds good resale value, it can be expensive, prone to creasing, and not the best choice for intense basketball play.

Description

The Air Jordan V, released in 1990, was designed to match Michael Jordan's aggressive playing style with features like a sharktooth design inspired by a WWII plane, translucent "iced" outsoles, and lace-lock toggles. The shoe also had a visible Air-Sole unit, mesh from earlier models, and an embroidered Jumpman logo with 3M reflectivity on the tongue. The ankle collar was designed to provide support while still being flexible, and the higher cut offered more protection. The Grape Purple/Emerald colorway was a first for a basketball shoe, bringing even more attention to Jordan's game. That season, Jordan made his sixth straight All-Star appearance, won his fourth consecutive scoring title, and was named to the All-NBA and All-Defensive First Teams, although the Bulls still couldn't beat Detroit in the conference finals.
